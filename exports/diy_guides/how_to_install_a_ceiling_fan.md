**Instructions:**

1. 1. Ensure power is OFF at the breaker and verified with a voltage tester.
2. 2. Unscrew any decorative nuts or screws holding the old fixture's canopy to the ceiling box.
3. 3. Carefully lower the old fixture, supporting its weight. You may need a helper for heavier fixtures.
4. 4. Disconnect the electrical wires: typically, twist off the wire nuts connecting the fixture's wires to the house wiring (black to black, white to white, bare copper/green to bare copper/green).
5. 5. Remove the old fixture completely and set it aside for disposal.

### Step 2: Inspect and Prepare Electrical Box

Ensure the ceiling has a fan-rated electrical box securely installed to support the weight and movement of the new ceiling fan.

**Instructions:**

1. 1. If your existing electrical box is fan-rated (usually metal, securely attached to a joist or a fan-rated brace, and stamped with 'Acceptable for Fan Support' or 'For Fan Support'), you can proceed to Step 3. If unsure, assume it's not fan-rated.
2. 2. If the box is not fan-rated, or if you're installing where no box exists, you must install a new fan-rated brace and box (e.g., Saf-T-Brace).
3. 3. For retrofit fan braces: Insert the brace into the ceiling hole. Rotate the brace until the feet are resting on the ceiling joists above the drywall. Use the included wrench or screwdriver to expand the brace, tightening it against the joists. The brace typically has a built-in electrical box that will be centered in the hole.
4. 4. For new work: If you have attic access, you can install a fan-rated box directly to a joist using appropriate screws, or install wood blocking between joists and then attach the box.
5. 5. Ensure the electrical box is flush with the ceiling and securely tightened. Any wobble will transfer to the fan.
6. 6. Pull the house electrical wires (black, white, ground) through the knockout in the new fan-rated box, leaving enough slack (about 6-8 inches) to work with.

### Step 5: Make Electrical Connections

Connect the fan's electrical wires to the house wiring, following standard color codes and safety practices.

**Instructions:**

1. 1. Carefully lift the fan motor assembly (from Step 3) and hang it onto the hook or tab on the mounting bracket. This allows you to work with the wires hands-free.

3. 3. Connect the Ground wires: Twist the bare copper or green ground wire from the house wiring together with the green or bare copper ground wire from the fan. Secure them with a wire nut. Tuck this connection into the electrical box.

## üîß Maintenance Tips

- Clean fan blades regularly with a soft, damp cloth to prevent dust buildup, which can cause wobbling and reduce efficiency.
- Periodically check the tightness of the blade screws and mounting hardware. Loose screws can lead to wobbling or noise.
- If the fan develops a wobble, use the balancing kit (usually included with the fan) to rebalance the blades. This often involves attaching small weights to the blades.
- For fans with light kits, replace bulbs with the correct wattage and type as specified by the manufacturer.
- If your fan has a remote control, replace batteries annually or when performance degrades.

## üîç Troubleshooting

**Problem:** Fan Not Working / Light Not On
**Solution:** Check the circuit breaker at the electrical panel. Ensure the wall switch is on. Verify all wire connections are secure inside the canopy. Check that light bulbs are properly installed and not burnt out. For remote-controlled fans, check remote batteries and ensure the receiver is correctly wired.

**Problem:** Fan Wobbles Excessively
**Solution:** Ensure all blade screws are tight. Check that the fan is securely mounted to the ceiling box and that the downrod pins are secure. Use the fan balancing kit (weights and clips) to rebalance the blades. Ensure blades are not warped.

**Problem:** Fan Makes Noise (Humming/Clicking)
**Solution:** Check that all screws on the fan motor, blades, and light kit are tight. Ensure the canopy is not touching the ceiling or mounting bracket. Verify the electrical box is securely mounted and not vibrating against joists. A slight hum can be normal for some fans, but excessive noise may indicate loose parts or a faulty motor.

**Problem:** Light Flickers
**Solution:** Ensure light bulbs are screwed in tightly. Check wiring connections to the light kit. If using a dimmer switch, ensure it's compatible with the fan's light kit (especially with LED lights).

**Problem:** Remote Control Not Working
**Solution:** Replace remote batteries. Ensure the remote receiver is correctly wired and not obstructed. Some remotes require pairing with the fan; refer to your fan's manual for pairing instructions.
